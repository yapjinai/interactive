on the Internet
q:
  • you seem to very rarely drop your flippant GFOTY persona, even in interviews -
    • the only time I’ve seen you appear sincere or serious was your apology online after something you’d said was perceived as racist.
    • I thought it was interesting that you made an exception during that particular controversy.
Is the refusal to drop your performer’s façade important to what you do ? (I’d be very honored if you chose to speak as Polly-Louisa rather than GFOTY when answering these questions!)
a: “
GFOTY is GFOTY and Polly is Polly, two completely different people.
There is always a time and place where one has to drop a personality, and if one has caused unintentional upset then that is probably a good time to be serious for a minute.
The problem about being two different people however, is that the outside world tend to want to hate Polly , and expect Polly to be some sort of egotistical, confident, evil bitch , which is pretty much far from the truth.
But there is always a constant battle in my brain and i do keep needing to remind myself that I am not this evil person people want to believe I am. With the whole controversy, it felt like people in my social group were almost enjoying it to some extent , like they were enjoying my fall , but you can't let stuff get in your way.
”
